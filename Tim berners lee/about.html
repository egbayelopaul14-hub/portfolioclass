Ten Facts About Tim Berners-Lee

  1. He was born in London, England.
  2. He graduated from the University of Oxford with a degree in physics.
  3. He invented the World Wide Web in 1989 while working with a degree at CERN.
  4. He created the first web browser and web server.
  5. He founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) in 1994.
  6. He is a strong advocate for net neutrality and open web standards.
  7. He was Knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in 2004.
  8. He received the Turing Award in 2016 for his contributions to computer science.
  9. He has authored several books on web technology and its impact on society.
  10. He continues to work on projects to improve the web, including the Solid project for decentralized data storage.
